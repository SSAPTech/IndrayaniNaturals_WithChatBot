Classic Pav Bhaji
Ingredients:
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 tomatoes, pureed
  • 2 potatoes, boiled and mashed
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (peas, carrots, cauliflower), boiled and mashed
  • 2 tbsp Pav Bhaji Masala
  • Salt to taste
  • 8 pav (bread rolls)
  • Fresh coriander for garnish
Method:
  1. Heat butter in a large pan. Sauté onions and ginger-garlic paste.
  2. Add tomato puree and cook until oil separates.
  3. Add Pav Bhaji Masala and salt. Mix well.
  4. Add mashed potatoes and mixed vegetables. Mix and mash everything together.
  5. Add a little water to get the desired consistency. Simmer for 10 minutes.
  6. Toast the pav with butter and serve hot with the bhaji, garnished with coriander.
Spicy Shev Bhaji
Ingredients:
  • 2 tbsp oil
  • 1 tsp mustard seeds
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tomato, pureed
  • 2 tbsp Shev Bhaji Masala
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 cup sev (thick gram flour noodles)
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander for garnish
Method:
  1. Heat oil in a pan. Add mustard seeds. When they splutter, add onions and sauté until golden.
  2. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook for a minute.
  3. Add tomato puree and cook until oil separates.
  4. Add Shev Bhaji Masala and salt. Mix well.
  5. Add water and bring the gravy to a boil.
  6. Just before serving, add the sev to the gravy. Garnish with coriander and serve immediately.
Aromatic Biryani
Ingredients:
  • 500g chicken or mixed vegetables
  • 2 cups basmati rice, soaked for 30 mins
  • 1 cup plain yogurt
  • 3 onions, thinly sliced and fried until golden
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 3 tbsp Biryani Masala
  • A pinch of saffron soaked in milk
  • Ghee or oil for cooking
  • Fresh mint and coriander leaves
Method:
  1. Marinate chicken/vegetables with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, Biryani Masala, and half the fried onions for at least 1 hour.
  2. Cook the marinated mixture until it's almost done.
  3. Parboil the soaked rice until it's 70% cooked. Drain it.
  4. Layer the cooked mixture and rice in a heavy-bottomed pot. Top with remaining fried onions, saffron milk, mint, and coriander.
  5. Cover and cook on low heat (dum) for 20-25 minutes.
  6. Gently fluff the rice and serve hot with raita.
Vegetable Pulav
Ingredients:
  • 1.5 cups basmati rice, washed and soaked
  • 2 tbsp ghee or oil
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 bay leaf, 2 cloves, 1-inch cinnamon stick
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 cups mixed vegetables (carrots, peas, beans, potatoes)
  • 1 tbsp Pulav Masala
  • 3 cups water or vegetable broth
  • Salt to taste
Method:
  1. Heat ghee/oil in a pot. Add cumin seeds and whole spices.
  2. Add onions and sauté until translucent. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook for a minute.
  3. Add mixed vegetables and sauté for 2-3 minutes.
  4. Add Pulav Masala and salt. Stir well.
  5. Drain the rice and add it to the pot. Sauté gently for a minute.
  6. Add water or broth, bring to a boil, then cover and simmer until the rice is cooked and water is absorbed.
Authentic Misal Pav
Ingredients:
  • 2 cups mixed sprouts (matki/moth beans), boiled
  • 2 onions, finely chopped
  • 1 tomato, pureed
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 3 tbsp Misal Masala
  • Oil, mustard seeds, curry leaves
  • For serving: Farsan, chopped onions, coriander, lemon wedges, and pav
Method:
  1. Heat oil, add mustard seeds and curry leaves. Add chopped onions and sauté until golden.
  2. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook for a minute. Add tomato puree and cook well.
  3. Add Misal Masala and cook for another minute.
  4. Add the boiled sprouts and 3 cups of water. Bring to a boil and simmer for 15 minutes. This is the 'usal'.
  5. A red oily layer called 'tari' or 'kat' will float on top.
  6. To serve, place the usal in a bowl, top with farsan, chopped onions, and coriander. Serve with the 'tari' on the side and pav.
Mutton Bhaji
Ingredients:
  • 500g mutton, bone-in, cut into pieces
  • 2 tbsp oil
  • 2 onions, finely chopped
  • 1 large tomato, chopped
  • 2 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 2-3 green chilies, chopped
  • 1/2 cup fresh coriander, chopped
  • 1/2 cup fresh coconut, grated (optional)
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 2 tsp red chili powder
  • 2 tbsp Indrayani Garam Masala (or any Maharashtrian goda masala)
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1 tsp cumin powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Water as needed
  • Fresh coriander for garnish
Method:
  1. Heat oil in a heavy-bottomed pan. Add onions and sauté until golden brown.
  2. Add ginger-garlic paste and green chilies. Sauté for a minute until fragrant.
  3. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until soft.
  4. Add turmeric, red chili powder, coriander powder, cumin powder, and salt. Mix well.
  5. Add mutton pieces and sear on high heat for 3-4 minutes.
  6. Add garam masala and grated coconut (if using). Mix and cook for 2 minutes.
  7. Add enough water to cover the mutton. Cover and cook on low heat until the mutton is tender (about 45-60 minutes), stirring occasionally. Add more water if needed.
  8. Once the mutton is cooked and the gravy is thick, add chopped coriander and mix well.
  9. Serve hot with bhakri, chapati, or steamed rice. Garnish with more fresh coriander.
